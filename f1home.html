Formula 1

Formula One, or F1, is the top class of international racing for open-wheel single-seater cars, sanctioned by the FIA. Since its debut in 1950, the FIA Formula One World Championship has been a premier racing series. The term "formula" refers to the rules all cars must follow, and a season consists of multiple races, known as Grands Prix, held on purpose-built circuits or closed public roads around the world...

History

Formula One began in 1946, with its first World Championship in 1950 at Silverstone, where Giuseppe Farina won over Juan Manuel Fangio. Fangio dominated early on with five titles until Michael Schumacher surpassed him in 2003. The Constructors' Championship started in 1958, and while Stirling Moss is a legendary driver, he never won a title. The sport evolved with innovations from teams like Alfa Romeo and Ferrari until non-championship events ended in 1983 due to rising costs.
